Poetry



Poetry is a huge part of who I am. This is one of the poems that I wrote about a girl who fled to the United States:



I sit silently in front of Mr. Collins,

Miserably seeing myself,

Missing a crown whom I value the most.


But soon after,

The bell’s ringing.


I dash into the bathroom,

Opening my lunch box

Hearing the laughs,

Teases,

Giggles,

Whispers.


Finally,

The bell’s ringing

I get out of my third home after Afghanistan and uncle’s house;

A place where I go every single day

During the recess.

Stepping into classroom,

As if I step on lava

Eyes of hate stared towards me

But I’ve come this far

So I carry on.

If I am a sun,

I can brighten someone’s day

If I am a candy,

Light and sweet on the tray

But I’m only a boy named “Gul”

I kid you not, my classmates called me a long-beak gull.
